## Design Context

### Users
Parents enrolling children in swim lessons and competitive swim families looking for team info and registration. They arrive at the site to get information about programs or to sign up. The experience needs to serve both the "browsing parent" doing research and the "ready-to-commit parent" who wants a fast, clear signup flow.

### Brand Personality
**Trusted + Energetic + Playful**

The site should simultaneously communicate safety/professionalism (parents need to trust you with their kids) and fun/energy (kids should want to be there). These aren't in tension — the best swim schools feel like both at once.

The emotional goal on first visit: "This place is legit and my kid will be safe" AND "This looks fun, my kid will love it."

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode only. No dark mode needed.
- **Framework**: UIKit 3 — keep and polish, don't replace.
- **Core brand color**: Pink accent `#FF4870` is a keeper. Build around it.
- **Current palette**: Navy `#1B1C26` (dark sections), primary blue `#3097D1`, muted backgrounds `#fcfcfd`, body text `#292C3D`/`#4F5260`.
- **Typography**: Raleway (headings) + PT Serif (selective). Loaded via Google Fonts.
- **Visual assets**: Sea life illustrations, real student/instructor photography, wave dividers, noise texture overlays.
- **Direction**: Evolve and polish the current design — not a redesign. Improve consistency, tighten spacing, refine details. The bones are good.

### Anti-references
- Overly corporate (stiff stock photos, blue-gray everything)
- Cheap/DIY (clip art, inconsistent spacing, default Bootstrap look)
- Generic fitness brand (aggressive typography, dark/moody, protein-shake energy)

### Design Principles

1. **Trust through craft**: Every polished detail — consistent spacing, aligned elements, smooth interactions — signals "we pay attention to details here too." Parents notice.
2. **Energy without chaos**: Use the pink accent, playful sea life illustrations, and photography to create warmth and fun. But keep layouts clean and information hierarchy clear. Energetic ≠ cluttered.
3. **UIKit, refined**: Work within UIKit 3's system. Don't fight it or bolt on conflicting patterns. Polish means better use of what's already there — tighter section rhythm, more intentional card usage, consistent component styling.
4. **Photography first, illustration second**: Real photos of real kids swimming build trust faster than anything. Sea life illustrations add personality as supporting elements, not primary content.
5. **Fast to scan, easy to act**: Parents are busy. Clear headings, visible CTAs, scannable program info. Every page should answer "what is this?" and "what do I do next?" within seconds.

### Accessibility
No strict WCAG target mandated, but improvements are welcome. At minimum maintain AA contrast ratios on text, ensure interactive elements have clear focus states, and keep tap targets comfortable on mobile.
